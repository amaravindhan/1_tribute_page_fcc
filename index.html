Mr. Kumaraswami Kamaraj

“ The King Maker of India ”

Here's a time line of Mr. Kamaraj's life:

  • 1903, 15th Jul - Born in Virudhunagar, Tamil Nadu.

  • 1914 - Dropped out of school to support his mother.

  • 1920 - He became active in politics, when he was 18.

  • 1921 - Organised public meetings at Virudhunagar for Congress leaders.

  • 1921, 21st Sep - Attended the public meeting and met Gandhi for the first time.

  • 1922 - Participated in, boycotting the visit of the Prince of Wales as part of the Non-Cooperation Movement

  • 1923-25 - Participated in the Nagpur Flag Satyagraha.

  • 1927 - Started the Sword Satyagraha in Madras and was chosen to lead the Neil Statue Satyagraha.

  • 1930 - Went to jail for two years for participating in the “Salt Satyagraha”.

  • 1937 - Entered the Assembly winning the Sattur seat in the election.

  • 1942 - Attended the All-India Congress Committee in Bombay and returned to spread propaganda material for the Quit India Movement.

  • 1954, 13th Apr - Became the Chief Minister of Madras Province and remained Chief Minister for three consecutive terms (15 years). During his tenure as Chief Minister, when the municipality of Virudhunagar provided a direct water connection to his house, he ordered it to be disconnected immediately as he did not want any special privileges. He refused to use the Z-level security that was provided to him as the CM of Tamil Nadu and instead travelled with just one police patrol vehicle. He did not marry, did not own any property and was never tempted by power.

      Some Important achievements as Chief Minister of TamilNadu are,

    1. He reopened 6000 schools, closed in the previous government by C.Rajagopalachari, citing financial reasons and reopened 12,000 more schools, so that poor rural students had to walk no more than three kilometres to their nearest school.

    2. No village remained without a primary school and no panchayat without a high school.

    3. He strived to eradicate illiteracy by introducing free and compulsory education up to the eleventh standard.

    4. He introduced the Free Midday Meal Scheme to provide at least one meal per day to the lakhs of poor school children.

    5. He introduced free school uniforms to weed out caste, creed and class distinctions among young minds.

    6. Major irrigation schemes were planned in his period. Dams and irrigation canals were built across higher Bhavani, Mani Muthar, Aarani, Vaigai, Amaravathi, Sathanur, Krishnagiri, Pullambadi, Parambikulam and Neyyaru among others.

    7. Farmers who had dry lands were given oil engines and electric pump sets on an instalment basis.

    8. He was known for his simplicity and integrity. He played a major role in developing the infrastructure of the Madras state, TamilNadu and worked to improve the quality of life of the needy and the disadvantaged.

  • 1963, 02nd Oct - Resigned from the post of the Chief Minister. He proposed that all senior Congress leaders should resign from their posts and devote all their energy to the re-vitalization of the Congress. This suggestion came to be known as the Kamaraj Plan.

  • 1963, 09th Oct - Was elected the President of Indian National Congress.

  • 1964 - After Nehru's death, he successfully navigated the party through turbulent times. As the president of INC, he refused to become the next Prime Minister himself and was instrumental in bringing to power of Prime Minister, Lal Bahadur Shastri.

  • 1966, Jan - US Vice-President Hubert Humphrey, called Kamaraj as “one of the greatest political leaders in all the countries of the free world”.

  • 1966 - Made Nehru's daughter, Indira Gandhi as next Prime Minister. For this role, he was widely acclaimed as the “Kingmaker” during the 1960s.

  • 1969 - The Congress split, he became the leader of the Indian National Congress (Organisation) (INC(O)) in Tamil Nadu.

  • 1975, 02nd Oct - He died at his home, which also was the 12th anniversary of his resignation. He was aged 72 and died in his sleep due to a heart attack. When he died, he left behind 130 rupees, 2 pairs of sandals, 4 shirts, 4 dhotis and a few books.

  • 1976 - Was awarded India's highest civilian honour, “The Bharat Ratna”.

  • 2003 - The Government of India released a commemorative coin on his birthday.

“One should not accept any post to which one could not do full justice”.

-- His principle
